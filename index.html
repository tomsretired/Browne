Welcome to Thomas Goodrich Browne's family history

Unfortunately, most people don't get interested in their family history until most of the older people in the family are gone and their stories with them. I am one of them.

My interest started a few years ago when I was digging in the bottom of an old piece of furniture and discovered some amazing family history. There were stacks of letters all neatly bound together. The first pile I saw were correspondence (perhaps you could call them "love" letters) between my great-grandfather Charlie and Ada, the woman who would become his bride in about 1892.

I also found a photograph of my great-great-grandfather in his Civil War uniform and several letters he had written his wife from the battlefield in 1862 - 1864.

Since then I have found many other items passed down from the generations before me. These range from quilts and hand-sewn pants to hand written music from a Vaudeville act to, of course, photographs and newspaper articles.

After searching through boxes of old pictures and documents and research on the Internet, I have been able to put together a snapshot of my family history.

Over the next days and months, I will be adding these letters and other items I have found. You may contact me at: browne <AT> macalester . edu
